Getting Started

Installation

1

Install Tampermonkey

Download the Tampermonkey extension for Chrome or Firefox. It's free and allows custom scripts to run on websites.

2

Add the script

Open Tampermonkey → click + to create a new script → paste the full contents of the script file → save with Ctrl+S.

3

Configure your data

Go to bwayrush.com. A 🎭 button will appear in the bottom-right corner. Click it and open My Data to fill in your personal details.

4

Select shows and launch

Check the shows you want to enter, then click 🚀 Open lotteries. Each lottery page will open in a new tab and auto-fill itself.

How it works

Platform behaviour

Broadway Direct Auto

  • Opens each available "ENTER NOW" lottery in sequence
  • Auto-fills all form fields (name, email, DOB, zip, country, tickets)
  • If no reCAPTCHA appears → clicks ENTER automatically
  • If reCAPTCHA appears → waits for you to solve it, then moves to the next show
  • Detects the SUCCESS modal and closes it automatically to continue
  • If no open lotteries found → shows a clear message explaining why

Lucky Seat Auto

  • Runs when you open any luckyseat.com/shows/... page
  • Selects performances based on your time-of-day filter
  • Weekend showtimes (Sat/Sun) are always all selected
  • Detects already-entered slots (shown in red) and skips them
  • Shows a message if all performances for your filter are already entered
  • Auto-submits if no captcha, otherwise prompts you to solve it

Best practices

Tips for more wins

🎯
Enter every day. Lotteries reset per performance. Entering consistently dramatically increases your odds over time.
📬
Watch your email closely. Winner windows are short — usually 60 minutes. If you miss it, your tickets may be released.
🌙
Evening shows have more competition. Try selecting "Solo mañanas" — matinée lotteries tend to have fewer entrants.
🎭
Enter multiple shows for the same time slot. You can win tickets to more than one performance per slot.
⚠️
Use your real name. Winners must show a matching photo ID at the box office. Nicknames will get your tickets cancelled.
🔄
Already entered Lucky Seat? Your latest entry automatically replaces the previous one — re-entering is safe.
FAQ

Questions & Answers

Is this script safe to use? Will I get banned? ›
The script runs entirely in your browser and doesn't store any data externally. It mimics normal human behavior — filling forms and clicking buttons. That said, lottery terms of service prohibit automated entries. Use it at your own discretion.
Why does it ask me to solve a reCAPTCHA sometimes? ›
reCAPTCHA is a bot-detection challenge that requires human interaction. It cannot be bypassed for free. When the script detects one, it fills everything else and waits for you to click "I'm not a robot."
The script didn't fill some fields — what happened? ›
Lottery sites update their HTML from time to time. If this happens, you can still fill missing fields manually before clicking ENTER.
Can I enter the same show twice? ›
On Broadway Direct, duplicate entries are disqualified. On Lucky Seat, your latest entry replaces the previous one automatically — re-entering is fine.
Lucky Seat says "already entered all performances" — what does that mean? ›
It means all the performances that match your time filter (morning/night) are already entered — they appear in red on the page. Check your email for results, or try a different show.
Broadway Direct shows no "ENTER NOW" buttons — what do I do? ›
A banner will appear explaining the reason: all lotteries may be Closed or Upcoming, you may have already entered, or the lottery hasn't opened yet. Check the schedule on the show page.
I won! What do I do next? ›
Check your email immediately — you'll have about 60 minutes to claim and pay for your tickets via credit card. On Broadway Direct you can also check status at lottery.broadwaydirect.com. On Lucky Seat, log into your account dashboard.
What happened to TodayTix and Hamilton? ›
TodayTix requires a native app for lottery entries. Hamilton's lottery is managed by its own platform with strong anti-bot protections. Both are excluded intentionally.
